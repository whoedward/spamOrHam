Upcoming Events
11/10-11/22

Visit www.northwestern.edu/onebook for more information.

 

 

 

Little Women: The Broadway Musical
11/7 - 11/23
7:30 PM (Thursday, Friday, Saturday)
2:00 PM (Sunday)
Josephine Louis Theater

A musical adaptation of Louis May Alcott's classic novel.

Purchase tickets here.

 

 

 

Deploying and Shattering Stereotypes: Panel Discussion
Wednesday, 11/12
6:30 pm
Block Museum of Art: Pick-Laudati Auditorium
FREE

Panel featuring Joy Bivins, Chicago History Museum curator; Maud Lavin, School of the Art Institute of Chicago professor; and Kathleen Bickford Berzock, the Block's Associate Director of Curatorial Affairs.

 

 

 

Creativity and the Arts
Wednesday, 11/12
12:00 - 1:00 pm
Wallis Theater (1949 Campus Dr.)
FREE

Ivonne Chand O'Neal, Director of Research and Evaluation for the John F. Kennedy Center for the performing arts, will discuss broad themes including the economics of creativity, creativity research and interdisciplinary themes of arts education.

 

 

 

Dinner and Discussion:
Applying Identity Contingencies and Stereotype Threats
Tuesday, 11/18
5:30 - 7:30 pm
Norris University Center: Dittmar Gallery
FREE
RSVP here.

Join One Book One Northwestern for another Dittmar Dinner and Discussion. School of Communications Professor Emeritus Paul Artnson will lead an open discussion on disability issues. We will examine if and how Claude Steele's analysis of identity contingencies and stereotype threats apply to people labeled physically, mentally, and cognitively disabled. Dinner is free and open to the public, but RSVP is required to attend. Space is limited and filled on a first come first serve basis.

 

 

 

NU Day at the RACE Exhibit
RACE: Are We So Different?
Saturday, 11/22
12:30 pm - 4:00 pm
Illinois Holocaust Museum
9603 Woods Drive, Skokie, IL 
FREE
RSVP here.

Developed by the American Anthropological Association in collaboration with the Science Museum of Minnesota, RACE is the first national exhibition to tell the stories of race from the biological, cultural, and historical points of view. Combining these perspectives offers an unprecedented look at race and racism in the United States.

NU Day at the RACE Exhibit will feature a talk by Noor A. Ali, Assistant Director of Multicultural Student Affairs. Free transportation will be provided by One Book.
